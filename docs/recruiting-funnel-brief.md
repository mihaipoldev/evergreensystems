# Recruiting Cold-Email Service — Content Brief

Everything we need to communicate on the sales page. This is the information and the copy — **how to lay it out, group it, and design it is up to you.** Build the structure and visual treatment however works best.

**The business:** A done-for-you cold-email lead-generation service. We build and run the entire outbound system that books qualified hiring-manager calls for boutique tech recruiting agency owners (US + Canada). The owner does nothing but show up to the calls.

**The audience:** Owners and managing partners of boutique, tech-focused recruiting agencies in the US or Canada, where the founder still personally runs business development. Not solo recruiters, not generalist staffing firms, not agencies that want guaranteed placements.

---

## The core promise
10–15 qualified hiring-manager calls in 90 days. The floor of 10 is guaranteed — miss it and every retainer dollar comes back (tool costs excluded).

## The pain we're speaking to
Recruiting agency owners can't run delivery and business development at the same time. When delivery loads up, BD stops. Six to eight weeks later the pipeline is dry, an anchor client pauses hiring, and revenue cliffs. They know the pattern and can't fix it themselves, because the same person who would do BD is the one buried in delivery.

## Why outbound, and why their other channels fail them
Recruiting agencies have three ways to find new clients: referrals, LinkedIn InMail, and cold outreach. Two of them break at the worst possible moment.
- Referrals dry up exactly when delivery is heavy and the owner stops showing up in the network.
- LinkedIn InMail has decayed — response rates fell from 20–25% to 6–8% over three years while seat costs keep rising.
- Cold outreach run from *outside* the business is the one channel that keeps producing through every delivery spike — because it doesn't depend on the owner's time.

The result: hiring-manager conversations at companies actively building tech teams, every month.

## Why most cold email fails in recruiting (and why ours doesn't)
Most cold email aimed at these agencies is the same: broad scraped lists, generic templates, candidate-side framing ("we'll fill your roles faster"), and replies that pile up because no one works the inbox. It fails for many reasons — wrong angle, no signal verification, broad lists, cold domains that land in spam, and positive replies that die unhandled.

We do the opposite: recruiting-only, BD-side angle, signal-verified prospects (companies with open engineering roles and recent hiring signals), one contact per company, and every reply handled by us end-to-end.

## What "enrichment" means (our edge)
Every prospect is researched before any message goes out. We verify: the decision-maker (VP Engineering, Head of Talent, CTO, or founder), funding stage and headcount growth, recent leadership changes, open engineering roles by stack and seniority, and recent buying signals (funded in the last 6 months, posted reqs in the last 30 days). Every email is based on information, not assumptions.

## How we protect their sender reputation
Outreach goes from dedicated secondary domains, never their main domain. Per-inbox volume is capped to keep sending natural. Domains are warmed for 21–28 days before any cold send. Every reply is handled by us. (Recruiting buyers are pitched daily by job boards, ATSes, and rival agencies, so deliverability discipline matters more here than anywhere.)

- Lead sourcing across VP Engineering, Head of Talent, CTOs, and founders at target accounts

What they DON'T do: manage tools, chase replies, or babysit campaigns. They just show up to the calls.

## The outcomes that change for them

## How the engagement works (the process)
1. Alignment and discovery — lock the ICP, sender voice, qualification criteria, and the conversations that move their business.
2. Infrastructure setup — secondary domains and inboxes purchased and configured, scaled to target volume, full authentication. Their main domain is never touched.
3. Warmup — 21–28 days, reputation built gradually so sends land in the inbox.
4. Prospect sourcing and enrichment — hiring decision-makers at funded tech companies sourced and enriched while inboxes warm. One contact per company.
5. Sequence build and reply handling — sequence written in their voice, personalization wired in, reply handling and booking flow configured.
6. Launch and optimization — sends go live, we handle every reply, qualified calls book to their calendar with a pre-call brief, performance reported and tuned continuously.

## Objections to answer (FAQ material)
- **"We get most clients through referrals — why do we need this?"** Referrals are the best clients they'll ever get, but they dry up exactly when delivery is heavy and they stop networking. This runs in parallel — they keep referrals, we add a channel that produces whether they're networking or not.
- **"We tried cold email before, didn't work."** Most stopped for one of two reasons: doing it themselves and quitting when delivery ramped, or hiring a generalist agency with niche-agnostic copy and broad lists. We're recruiting-only, signal-verified, and don't stop when delivery ramps.
- **"Hiring managers get pitched constantly — why would they respond?"** Most pitches are candidate-side ("I'll fill your role faster"). Ours is BD-side and hits a real pain. Recruiting is sales-driven; these buyers respond to targeted, relevant outreach. One contact per company roughly doubles reply rates vs. blasting a leadership team.
- **"We're already doing some BD — does this replace it?"** Either. Most agencies have tried an in-house BDR, a fractional sales lead, or their own LinkedIn outreach. Those all stop when delivery ramps because they need someone inside the business with time. We don't — so we replace what isn't working or run alongside what is.
- **"Tech companies are bringing recruiting in-house — why invest in BD now?"** Real concern since the 2025 layoffs. But the agencies that survive deliver specialized roles in-house teams can't fill (AI/ML, staff+ IC, security, niche stacks), and the ones that grow fixed their BD before the market shrank. A shrinking market is exactly why investing in BD now matters — whoever shows up takes share.
- **"Does this take time away from delivery?"** Zero owner time after kickoff. We handle everything before the call; they just show up, with a pre-call brief so they walk in knowing the company, the open roles, and the angle.
- **"Do you source candidates too?"** No. Pure BD-side. We book hiring-manager conversations; the candidate side is their domain and we don't dilute it.
